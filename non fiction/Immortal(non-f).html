NON FICTION

Author : Rebecca Skloot

"The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks" is a non-fiction book written by Rebecca Skloot. Published in 2010, it tells the story of Henrietta Lacks, an African-American woman whose cancer cells were taken without her knowledge in 1951. These cells, known as HeLa cells, became the first immortal human cell line and played a significant role in medical research and scientific advancements. The book explores the ethical, social, and scientific implications of Henrietta Lacks' story, highlighting the lack of informed consent and the broader issues of bioethics and medical privacy. It's a thought-provoking account that delves into the intersection of science, race, and ethics.
