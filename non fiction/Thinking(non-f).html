Author : Daniel Kahneman

"Thinking, Fast and Slow" is a renowned book written by Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman. Published in 2011, the book explores the two systems that drive how humans think: System 1, which is fast, automatic, and intuitive, and System 2, which is slower, deliberate, and analytical. Kahneman delves into cognitive biases, heuristics, and the complexities of decision-making, providing insights into why people often make irrational choices. The book is a seminal work in the fields of psychology, behavioral economics, and decision theory, offering readers a deep understanding of the way our minds work and the factors that influence our judgments and choices.
