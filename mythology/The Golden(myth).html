MYTHOLOGY

Author : James George Frazer

"The Golden Bough" is a seminal work of anthropology and comparative religion written by Sir James George Frazer. First published in 1890, the book explores the mythologies, rituals, and beliefs of various cultures, focusing on the theme of the dying and resurrecting god. It delves into the concept of magic, religion, and the evolution of human thought. Frazer's work has had a significant influence on the fields of anthropology and folklore studies, and it continues to be studied for its insights into the connections between different cultural practices and beliefs.
